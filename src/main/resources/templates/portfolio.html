Architectural Principles

Mental models and core philosophies that guide my engineering decisions

Principle 01

Design for Failure

Internet and computers will fail sometimes. I build software that automatically saves your work, retries failed actions, and keeps your data safe when things unexpectedly go wrong.

Principle 02

Follow the Data

I carefully track how information moves through the app from the user's screen out to the secure database, making sure nothing is ever lost, hacked, or slowed down along the way.

Principle 03

Built for Tanzania

Building for our environment means engineering apps that still work perfectly on cheaper phones, with low battery, and very slow internet connections—without ever losing quality.

Principle 04

No Money Lost

When dealing with money, losing a single record is absolutely unacceptable. I write code that double-checks every payment and calculation to make sure everything is 100% accurate.

Principle 05

Silent Observers

I build automatic monitoring tools into my apps so I get alerts about hidden problems. This lets me fix issues quickly, long before the business owner or customers even notice.

Principle 06

Keep It Clean

I write clean and organized code. This makes it super easy for other developers or companies to join my work, understand what I built, and add new features safely.
